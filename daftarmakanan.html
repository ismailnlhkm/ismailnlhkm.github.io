1. Telur

Eggs include food sources of protein, fat, and micronutrients such as vitamin B complex and choline. What is special about egg protein is that it is of high quality. This is because egg protein is composed of a variety of amino acids, particularly leucine. Leucine is one of the essential amino acids for building muscle. These amino acids play a direct role in the formation of proteins, cell division, and metabolism that takes place in your muscles.



2. Soybeans

Soybeans are the right choice if you want to build muscle. One hundred grams of cooked soybeans contains 15 grams of protein which is equivalent to 25% of the daily requirement of adults. In addition, there is also a content of iron, vitamin K, and phosphorus. Vegetable protein sources usually do not contain complete amino acids. However, it is different with soybeans. These foods contain all kinds of amino acids, especially leucine which is needed for building muscle mass.



3. Yogurt

Dairy products such as yogurt contain two kinds of protein, namely whey protein which is quickly digested and casein which is slow to digest. Consumption of these two proteins together is believed to increase muscle mass more quickly. In addition, yogurt contains vitamin D which helps the body absorb calcium and phosphorus. Calcium plays an important role in muscle contraction, while phosphorus is needed to form adenosine triphosphate (ATP) as a source of energy for your muscles.


4. Milk

Milk contains lots of vitamins, minerals, quality animal protein, and healthy fats. In addition, milk is one of the best sources of calcium. To get the nutrients found in milk, you can consume it directly or consume processed products, such as cheese and yogurt.



5. Chicken

Meat is one of the healthiest foods because it is rich in nutrients, especially lean beef. This type of meat contains the highest protein and iron compared to other types of meat. In addition to meat, eggs are also included in a healthy diet. In addition to being high in protein, eggs also contain complete nutrients, ranging from vitamins, minerals, unsaturated fats, to antioxidants.




6. Fruits

In addition to vegetables, fruit is also very important to eat because it contains many vitamins. Some of the fruits that are popular for their nutritional richness are:
Apples (fiber, vitamin C, and antioxidants)
Oranges (vitamin C)
Bananas (potassium)
Berries, such as blueberries and strawberries (low in calories but high in antioxidants and fiber)
